Theory Notes
📡

1. WiFi Uses Radio Waves

WiFi transmits data as radio waves — the same type of electromagnetic wave used by FM radio, TV broadcasts, and mobile phones, but at a much higher frequency. Your router converts digital data (0s and 1s) into radio waves, broadcasts them through its antenna, and your device's wireless chip receives and decodes them.

Radio waves travel at the speed of light and can pass through air, walls, and most building materials — though solid objects reduce signal strength.

📶

2. Two Frequency Bands

⚠️ Critical for ESP32-CAM

2.4 GHz

  • 2,400,000,000 waves/sec
  • Range up to ~45m indoors
  • Passes through walls better
  • More interference (shared with microwaves, Bluetooth)
  • Slower data speed
✅ ESP32-CAM ONLY supports 2.4 GHz

5 GHz

  • 5,000,000,000 waves/sec
  • Range up to ~20m indoors
  • Blocked by walls more easily
  • Less interference (fewer devices)
  • Faster data speed
❌ ESP32-CAM cannot connect to 5 GHz
🌐

3. What Does a Router Do?

A router creates a Local Area Network (LAN) — a private network that devices can join to communicate with each other. When your phone sends a motor command to the ESP32-CAM, the data travels as radio waves to the router, and the router forwards it to the car. No internet is required for this — it all happens locally.

Our project uses a dedicated router with no internet connection. This means students keep their WiFi and the cars all work without school network restrictions.

⚠️

4. What Reduces WiFi Signal?

FactorWhy It Matters for the Spy Car
DistanceThe further the car drives from the router, the weaker the signal. Video stream becomes choppy before dropping out.
Walls & obstaclesMetal benches, concrete walls, and filing cabinets all absorb 2.4 GHz signals. Drive around obstacles, not through them.
Too many devices20 ESP32-CAMs on one router = 20 devices competing for bandwidth. Use QVGA resolution instead of VGA in class.
InterferenceMicrowaves, Bluetooth speakers, and other WiFi networks all share 2.4 GHz. Move the router away from these devices.
Wrong frequencyIf the router broadcasts only 5 GHz, the ESP32-CAM will try for 15 seconds then fail with a slow LED flash.
Reference — Design & Technology

Component Guide

Know what every part does before you pick up a wire. You are responsible for not damaging components.

📷

ESP32-CAM (AI Thinker)

The brain of the car. A microcontroller with a built-in camera and WiFi radio. Runs a web server, streams MJPEG video on port 81, and accepts motor commands on port 80.

GPIO 13, 14, 15, 12 → L298N
⚡

L298N Motor Controller

The H-Bridge. Takes low-power logic signals (3.3V) from the ESP32 and switches high-current motor power. Also has an onboard 5V regulator to power the ESP32 from the battery.

IN1–IN4 ← ESP32 | OUT1–4 → Motors
🔧

TT DC Gearbox Motor × 2

200 RPM, 3–6V DC motors. One drives each rear wheel. Direction is controlled by the L298N. No PWM needed — ENA/ENB jumpers stay ON for full speed.

OUT1/OUT2 + OUT3/OUT4 from L298N
🔵

Omniwheel (Front)

A passive front castor wheel that can rotate in any direction. Supports the chassis and allows smooth turning with the two rear-drive motors.

No electrical connection — structural only
🔋

Pololu 6×AA Battery Holder

6 × 1.5V AA batteries = 9V. Single power supply for the entire car. Has an inline on/off switch. Powers motors via L298N and the ESP32 via L298N's 5V regulator pin.

+ → L298N 12V screw | − → GND
📦

Cardboard Box Chassis

Your structural frame. Lightweight, easy to cut and modify. Hold components with hot glue or cable ties. Design it so the battery switch is accessible from outside.

No electrical connection — structural only
Power Chain
💡
ONE battery pack powers everything. Here is the flow:

Battery → Switch → L298N 12V input → Motors (via H-Bridge)
Battery → L298N onboard regulator → 5V pin → ESP32-CAM
⚠️
Safety Rule: Always turn the battery switch OFF before making any wiring changes. Motors can spin unexpectedly when power is applied. Never connect or disconnect wires while the switch is ON.

Upload Sequence
  1. Change WIFI_SSID, WIFI_PASS, and CAR_NAME at the top of the sketch.
  2. Bridge GPIO0 to GND on the ESP32-CAM (hold LOW during upload).
  3. Power on the board, then click Upload in Arduino IDE.
  4. Wait for "Done uploading" in the console.
  5. Remove the GPIO0–GND bridge, then press the Reset button.
  6. Open Serial Monitor at 115200 baud and wait for the IP address.
  7. Make sure your device is on the same WiFi network, then open the IP in Chrome.
